Your Complete Guide to
Disneyland Resort

Two incredible parks. Seventy years of magic. Here's everything your family needs to know — from a dad who's done the homework so you don't have to.

✨ Plan My Disneyland Trip

Disneyland Resort in Anaheim, California is where Walt Disney's dream began in 1955 — and today it's home to two amazing theme parks, three Disney-owned resort hotels, the Downtown Disney District, and over 50 Good Neighbor Hotels nearby. Skip the Line

Lightning Lane Explained

Disney's paid line-skip system gives you three options depending on your budget and how you want to spend your day. Every ride still has a free standby queue — Lightning Lane is completely optional, but can save significant time on busy days.

LLMP

Lightning Lane Multi Pass

Book arrival windows one at a time for included attractions at both parks. After scanning in, you can immediately book your next selection. Includes photo downloads.

LLSP

Lightning Lane Single Pass

Purchased separately for the highest-demand rides not in the Multi Pass. At Disneyland: Star Wars: Rise of the Resistance. At DCA: Radiator Springs Racers. Up to 2 per day.

LLPP

Lightning Lane Premier Pass

Covers all Multi Pass AND Single Pass attractions at both parks, all day, no return windows needed. Just walk up and ride. Limited availability — purchase up to 2 days in advance.

💡 Key Disneyland Lightning Lane Differences vs. Walt Disney World

  • At Disneyland, one Multi Pass purchase covers both parks (DCA included)
  • Multi Pass selections can only be made after entering a park on the day of your visit
  • Premier Pass covers both parks in a single purchase — great value vs. WDW
  • Hotel guests receive one complimentary Multi Pass Lightning Lane use per stay
  • Rise of the Resistance is the top Single Pass ride — book it the moment you enter
  • Popular rides like Indiana Jones & Radiator Springs Racers sell out fast — plan early

Crowd Calendar

Timing Is Everything

Disneyland draws visitors year-round, but picking the right week makes a huge difference in wait times and enjoyment. Local school schedules and Southern California residents drive much of the crowd fluctuation.

January
🟢 Best

Post-holiday calm. Low crowds, short waits. Great deals on hotels.

February
🔵 Good

Valentine's week and President's Day weekend bring some crowds, but generally manageable.

March
🟡 Busy

Spring break season ramps up mid-month. Watch out for school breaks.

April
🔴 Avoid

Spring break peak. One of the busiest periods of the year, especially around Easter.

May
🟢 Best

Late April–May is a sweet spot. Schools in session, weather is warm, crowds are lighter.

June
🟡 Busy

Summer begins. Crowds build fast as school lets out across the country.

July
🔴 Avoid

Peak summer crowds. 4th of July week is among the most crowded of the year.

August
🟡 Busy

Still busy. Late August starts to ease as schools return in other states.

September
🔵 Good

Post-Labor Day is a hidden gem. Halloween season starts late September with Oogie Boogie Bash.

October
🔵 Good

Halloween Time is incredible. Weekdays are manageable; weekends get busy for Oogie Boogie Bash.

November
🟡 Busy

Thanksgiving week is one of the most crowded periods. Early November is quieter.

December
🔴 Avoid

Holiday magic is stunning, but crowds are intense — especially Dec. 26–Jan. 1.

🎃

Oogie Boogie Bash

Disney California Adventure's separately-ticketed Halloween party runs select nights in September and October. Includes special character meets, trick-or-treating, and exclusive entertainment. Tickets sell out fast — book as early as possible.

🎄

Holiday Time at Disneyland

From early November through early January, the resort transforms with holiday décor, seasonal overlays on beloved attractions, and the beloved Candlelight Processional. Stunning, but plan around high crowds.

🎆

Disneyland's 70th Anniversary

2025 marks Disneyland's 70th anniversary, bringing special entertainment, new attractions, and exclusive experiences. The celebration continues into 2026 — an incredible time to visit.

Accommodations

Where to Stay

Disney Resort Hotels

Staying on property puts you fully inside the Disney bubble — and comes with exclusive perks, including one complimentary Lightning Lane Multi Pass entry per guest per stay (as of January 2025).

Grand Californian Hotel & Spa

Deluxe

Disney's crown jewel in Anaheim. This Arts & Crafts-style lodge has a private entrance directly into Disney California Adventure and Downtown Disney. Character dining at Storytellers Café. Pool complex with waterslide. The ultimate Disneyland stay.

Best for: Families wanting full immersion & luxury

Disneyland Hotel

Deluxe

The original Disneyland hotel — reimagined and reopened in 2023 with stunning new theming inspired by classic Disney attractions. Short walk to the parks. Fantasy Tower rooms feature Disney décor throughout.

Best for: Disney fans who want classic magic

Pixar Place Hotel

Moderate

Formerly Paradise Pier Hotel, now fully reimagined with Pixar theming throughout. Great location, more affordable than its sister properties, with the same resort perks including the complimentary Lightning Lane benefit.

Best for: Families who want on-site perks at lower cost

Good Neighbor Hotels

Disneyland's Good Neighbor Hotel program features over 50 hotels hand-picked by Disney for quality, comfort, and proximity to the parks — all within about a mile of the resort gates. You can book them through the official Disneyland website and bundle tickets into a vacation package.

Note: Good Neighbor Hotels do not include the complimentary Lightning Lane benefit, but they are significantly more affordable than Disney-owned hotels and many have complimentary offers

Good Neighbor Package Perks (when booked through Disney) — When you book a Good Neighbor Hotel vacation package through Walt Disney Travel Company, your package includes park tickets, a collectible pin & lanyard, and 10% off at many participating Downtown Disney dining and merchandise locations. Food & Dining

Where to Eat at Disneyland Resort

🏆

Signature Dining

Blue Bayou Restaurant — the iconic waterside restaurant inside Pirates of the Caribbean. Reserve as early as possible. Carthay Circle in DCA for a refined California dining experience. Napa Rose at the Grand Californian for fine dining.

👑

Character Dining

Storytellers Café at Grand Californian Hotel — breakfast and lunch with Disney Characters. Goofy's Kitchen at Disneyland Hotel — all-day dining with a revolving cast of characters including Goofy, Pluto, and friends.

🍕

Quick-Service Favorites

Ronto Roasters (Galaxy's Edge) for Ronto Wraps, Oga's Cantina for Star Wars-themed drinks, Award Wieners (DCA), Shawarma Palace (Avengers Campus), and Alien Pizza Planet in Tomorrowland.

🛍️

Downtown Disney

The free-admission district outside the parks has grown significantly. Earl of Sandwich, Black Tap Craft Burgers, Salt & Straw ice cream, and the always-buzzing Ballast Point Brewing & Spirits are popular stops.

🥐

Must-Try Snacks

Monte Cristo Sandwich at Blue Bayou, Mickey-shaped beignets in New Orleans Square, Dole Whip in Adventureland, Churros throughout the park, and the famous Corn Dogs from the Little Red Wagon on Main Street.

📱

Dining Reservations

Reservations can be made up to 60 days in advance through the Disneyland app or website. Blue Bayou, Napa Rose, and Carthay Circle book up fast — reserve the moment your window opens. Quick-service requires no reservation.

Know Before You Go

What's Included vs. Extra Cost

✅ Included with Park Admission

  • Access to all standard attractions in both parks (with valid park reservation)
  • Live entertainment — parades, street performers, character cavalcades
  • Nighttime spectaculars — World of Color, fireworks, Fantasmic!
  • Character meet & greets (standard)
  • Downtown Disney District access (no ticket required)
  • Play Disney Parks app interactive experiences throughout Galaxy's Edge
  • Park Hopper (if added to ticket) — cross between parks from 11 AM
  • Free standby queues for every ride

+ Costs Extra

  • Lightning Lane Multi Pass
  • Lightning Lane Single Pass
  • Lightning Lane Premier Pass
  • Savi's Workshop — Build a Lightsaber
  • Droid Depot — Build a Droid
  • Oga's Cantina (reservation recommended, walk-in sometimes available)
  • Bibbidi Bobbidi Boutique — princess and knight makeovers
  • Special ticketed events (Oogie Boogie Bash, Mickey's Halloween Party)
  • All food, beverages, and merchandise
  • Parking

From The Imagineer Dad

8 Pro Tips for Disneyland

1

Book dining reservations 60 days out. Blue Bayou and Napa Rose fill up within hours of the booking window opening. Set an alarm, open the Disneyland app at 6 AM Pacific, and book the moment you can.

2

Start in Disney California Adventure. DCA tends to be less crowded at rope drop. Knock out Radiator Springs Racers or Guardians early, then head to Disneyland once lines build there.

3

Rise of the Resistance is worth the Single Pass. It's one of the best theme park experiences anywhere in the world. The wait on a busy day can exceed 90 minutes.

4

Use the Disneyland app from the moment you enter. Book your first Lightning Lane selection immediately. You can make your next selection as soon as you scan in — make the most of every minute.

5

Stay on-property if it fits your budget. The Grand Californian's private DCA entrance alone can save 15–20 minutes of walking per park trip — and that complimentary Lightning Lane entry is a real perk over multi-day stays.

6

Galaxy's Edge is magical at night. The land comes to life after dark with glowing lights and projection shows on the spires. If you visit during the day, plan a return after sunset.

7

Weekdays beat weekends — always. If you have any flexibility, visiting Tuesday through Thursday makes a noticeable difference. Local Southern California residents crowd the parks on weekends year-round.

8

Park reservations are required. Even with a valid ticket, you must also have a park reservation for each day. Book as soon as you purchase your tickets — availability, especially on peak days, can be limited.
