Know Before You Go

The 4 Theme Parks

Each park has a completely different feel, pacing, and target audience. Knowing what each offers helps you prioritize your days and energy.

The Classic

Magic Kingdom

The most iconic park in the world — home to Cinderella Castle, the most rides of any WDW park, and the purest Disney magic. Best for families with young kids and first-time visitors. Plan a full day here minimum.

  • Seven Dwarfs Mine Train
  • TRON Lightcycle / Run
  • Space Mountain
  • Haunted Mansion
  • Pirates of the Caribbean
  • Festival of Fantasy Parade
  • Happily Ever After Fireworks
  • Cinderella's Royal Table (dining)
Food, Culture & Thrills

EPCOT

Half futuristic innovation, half global exploration — EPCOT's World Showcase visits 11 countries with unique food, drinks and entertainment. A grown-up favorite that also has some of the best rides on property.

  • Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind
  • Frozen Ever After
  • Test Track
  • Soarin' Around the World
  • Remy's Ratatouille Adventure
  • Luminous the Symphony of Us (nighttime)
  • Kidcot Fun Stops
Action & Star Wars

Hollywood Studios

Home to the best thrill rides in all of WDW — Rise of the Resistance and Slinky Dog Dash alone make a day here essential. Toy Story Land and Galaxy's Edge are immersive worlds within the park.

  • Star Wars: Rise of the Resistance
  • Slinky Dog Dash
  • Tower of Terror
  • Rock 'n' Roller Coaster starring The Muppets
  • Mickey & Minnie's Runaway Railway
  • Indiana Jones Epic Stunt Spectacular
  • Fantasmic! (nighttime)
Nature & Adventure

Animal Kingdom

A lush, immersive nature and adventure park — the most beautiful of the four parks. Pandora is breathtaking at night. Plan an early start as animals are most active in the morning and the park often closes earlier.

  • Avatar: Flight of Passage
  • Na'vi River Journey
  • Expedition Everest
  • Kilimanjaro Safaris
  • Festival of The Lion King
  • Finding Nemo: The Big Blue...and Beyond!
  • Tusker House Restaurant

🎡 The Imagineer Dad's Park Order Recommendation

For most families visiting WDW for the first time, here's the order I recommend and why:

  • Day 1: Magic Kingdom — start with the most iconic, energy is high. It is the bigger of all the parks with the most to do.
  • Day 2: EPCOT — slower pace, great food, perfect midweek park
  • Day 3: Rest day: Pool day or Disney Springs — build it in, you'll need it
  • Day 4: Hollywood Studios — Get a Bounty in Star Wars after a relaxing day poolside
  • Day 5: Animal Kingdom — morning safari, Pandora at dusk, best lighting
  • Park Hopper? Use it to hit EPCOT evenings for Luminous after dinner, or fireworks again at Magic Kingdom

Skip the Lines

Lightning Lane Explained

Disney's line-skipping system replaced the old Genie+ and FastPass+ in 2024. There are now three tiers — here's what each one means and whether it's worth it for your family.

LLMP

Lightning Lane Multi Pass

Book one ride at a time with a return window. After riding, book your next one. Covers most popular rides. Purchase from the My Disney Experience app.

~$15–$30/person/day
LLSP

Lightning Lane Single Pass

One-time Lightning Lane access for the top tier headliners — Rise of the Resistance, TRON, Guardians. Purchased separately, per ride.

~$10–$25/person/ride
LLPP

Lightning Lane Premier Pass

Skip every Lightning Lane in one park with no return window needed. No scheduling, just walk up. Pricey but maximum flexibility for big spenders.

~$129–$449/person/day

⚡ The Imagineer Dad's Lightning Lane Tips

  • If you are going to get LLMP, Buy LLMP as soon as the park opens — availability drops fast
  • Book your first ride immediately at 7am on the app (resort guests) or park open
  • Use LLSP for Rise of the Resistance and TRON — waits hit 90+ minutes
  • Rope drop (arrive before park opens) and hit headliners first without LL
  • LLPP is a must for Magic Kingdom
  • Midday break at the resort + return in the evening = shorter evening waits

Accommodations

Where to Stay at WDW

Staying on-property unlocks perks that off-site guests don't get — including early park entry, free transportation, and free water park access on check-in day. Here's the breakdown by tier:

Value Resorts

Budget-friendly with all the on-property perks. Great for families who plan to spend most of their time in the parks. Pools are fun and themed.

Pop Century, All-Star Sports, Art of Animation

Moderate Resorts

Better theming, larger pools, table-service dining, and a more resort-like feel. Great middle ground for families wanting more comfort without the luxury price.

Port Orleans, Caribbean Beach, Coronado Springs

Deluxe Resorts

Monorail or boat access to Magic Kingdom, multiple dining options, stunning theming, and signature amenities. Worth it for honeymooners and special occasions.

Grand Floridian, Polynesian, Wilderness Lodge, Beach Club

Food & Restaurants

Dining at Walt Disney World

WDW has over 300 dining locations. The best table-service restaurants book up 60 days in advance — don't wait. Here's what to know:

👑

Signature Dining (Must Book Early)

Be Our Guest, Cinderella's Royal Table, Topolino's Terrace, Victoria & Albert's. These require reservations 60 days out and sell out fast — book the morning your window opens.

🥞

Character Dining

Meet characters while you eat — no waiting in line. Chef Mickey's, Tusker House, Topolino's breakfast, and Garden Grill are family favorites. Book early, especially for character breakfasts.

🌍

EPCOT World Showcase

11 countries, 11 unique dining experiences. San Angel Inn in Mexico, Chefs de France, Teppan Edo in Japan, and Via Napoli pizza are standouts. Also perfect for festival food booths.

🎪

EPCOT Festivals

WDW runs food and culture festivals almost year-round at EPCOT. Festival of the Arts (Jan–Feb), Flower & Garden (Mar–May), Food & Wine (Aug–Nov), and Festival of the Holidays (Nov–Dec).

🏰

Disney Springs

The shopping and dining district has celebrity chef restaurants — Morimoto Asia, STK, Wine Bar George — that don't require park tickets. Great for arrival night dinner or a day off from the parks.

🍦

Must-Try Snacks

Dole Whip at Magic Kingdom, LeFou's Brew in Fantasyland, Churros in Frontierland, School Bread in EPCOT's Norway pavilion, and a Butterbeer at Hogsmeade if you're hopping to Universal.

The Imagineer Dad's Park Advice

Pro Tips for a Perfect Walt Disney World Trip

1

Rope drop every morning. Arrive at the park entrance 30–45 minutes before official open. The first hour has the shortest waits of the entire day — you can knock out 2–3 headliners before the crowds arrive.

2

Book dining 60 days out — to the minute. Popular restaurants like Be Our Guest and Cinderella's Royal Table release at 6am EST exactly 60 days before your trip. Set an alarm. They're gone within minutes. Or Ask me to book it

3

Take a midday break. Go back to your resort from 1–4pm — it's the hottest, most crowded time of day. Swim, nap, recharge. Return for the evening when crowds thin and shows begin. It changes the whole trip.

4

Download My Disney Experience before you arrive. This is your command center for everything — Lightning Lane purchases, dining reservations, wait times, park maps, and mobile ordering food to skip counter lines.

5

Stay on-property if your budget allows. Early park entry alone is worth it — you get 30 minutes before the general public every single day. Over a 7-day trip that's 3+ extra hours of low-wait riding.

6

Visit Animal Kingdom early, leave early. The park often closes earlier than the others and the animals are most active in the morning. Plan this park as your shortest day or pair it with an afternoon at EPCOT if you have a Park Hopper.

7

Don't skip the nighttime shows. Happily Ever After at Magic Kingdom, Fantasmic at Hollywood Studios, and Luminous at EPCOT are genuinely spectacular. Plan your last night around whichever park is hosting the show you most want to see.

8
